Growing up in Oswego, I graduated from Oswego High School with my High School Diploma. I then moved on to attend Iowa State University, majoring in Chemical Engineering. I was there for two years before I decided to withdraw in my third fall semester and come home to change majors.

After I came home, I decided I wanted to go into computer science, and I took a few classes at Waubonsee Community College to get a feel for the major. In the fall of 2019, I transferred to Aurora University, majoring in Software Engineering and Computer Science, expected to graduate in spring of 2022

My senior year of High School I began working as a lifeguard for the Oswego Park District. Besides saving lives, I helped patrons with their questions and problems, as well as helped train newer lifeguards. I worked there for four years before getting a job at one of the dining centers at Iowa State University.

I only worked in the dining center for a few months before I withdrew from Iowa State. When I came home I got a job at the Meijer in Oswego. I worked in the produce department, mostly in the back cutting fruit for their pre-made fruit sets. I also helped stock the floor and answered customer requests and questions.

I worked at Meijer for a year before I got a job as a peer tutor at Aurora University. I tutored mainly for the intro to programming class, although the students had questions about other classes I had already taken. I tutored for a year, halfway through I also started working at the Portillos in Oswego. There I mostly worked in the kitchen, sometimes in the drivethrough bringing food to customers. I worked there for almost a year before having to move, and I am currently looking for another job.

My full name is Jared Thomas Reichardt. I was born in Oswego, Il, and spent most of my life growing up there. I was an active person growing up, learning piano and percusssion, participating in various athletics, and staying in school. In High School I ran cross country, was in band, marching band, and multiple honor societies. I also was in Boy Scouts, earning my Eagle Scout and becoming a leader within my own troop.

In College I continued my participation in band and other clubs and organizations I was interested, some being school related and others being for fun (i.e. the video gaming club). I stopped participating in marching band when I broke my arm in my second year, and all other involvement at Iowa State ended when I withdrew. At Aurora University I have a long commute, so the computer science club is all I am involved with. I still go for the occasional run, and enjoy hanging out with friends.
